Here are five facts about internet copyright law you may not have known about

What is protected by copyright in the World Wide Web?

Links, origional text, audio,and video on a webpage are all protected with copyright laws. source https://mason.gmu.edu/~montecin/copyright-internet.htm

What is the punishment for copyright infringment on the internet?

Punishment can be up to $150,000 fine for every work that was infringed. source https://copyrightalliance.org/ca_faq_post/do-copyright-laws-apply-on-the-internet/

Is there a law that protects authors of digital software from me downloading their work for free?

DMCA for short, the Digital Millenium Copyright Act of 1998 protects authors of digital software from being infringed upon. source: https://www.eff.org/issues/dmca

Why am i able to use websites like the Pirate Bay? Why dont I get prosecuted?

It is tough to catch individuals who illegaly download files in small quantities. The co-founders of the Pirate Bay was sentenced one year in jail, and fined $3.6 million in 2009. Source https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2009/apr/17/the-pirate-bay-trial-guilty-verdict

If I alter the work of another individual, or only use part of it, is that considered fair use?

Derivatives of work are still covered by copyright. The only exception is if you are writing a review or critique. Reacting to, or doing a parody of the work could also fall under this. source https://legal123.com.au/how-to-guide/copyright-infringement-myths-vs-facts-infographic/

If you dont learn internet copyright laws you could be in serious trouble.

Using content from other people on the internet for educational pourposes can still be infringement. It depends on how much of the content was used, and other factors.
